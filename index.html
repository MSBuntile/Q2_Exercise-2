Momentum

a property of a moving body that the body has by virtue of its mass and motion and that is equal to the product of the body's mass and velocity.

Momentum = mass x velocity

p = m x v

Units: kg m/s

Valence Electrons

a single electron or one of two or more electrons in the outer shell of an atom that is responsible for the chemical properties of the atom

How to find valence electrons?

1. Find a periodic table of elements.

2. Label each column on the periodic table of elements from 1 to 18.

3. Use the group numbers to determine the number of valence electrons.

Force

an agency or influence that if applied to a free body results chiefly in an acceleration of the body and sometimes in elastic deformation and other effects

Other effects of force are:

- Decelerate an object

- Change the direction of motion of a body

- Change the shape and size of a body
